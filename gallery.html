A major city province of Zambales in the Philippines, Olongapo has a growing population of 260,000 as per the 2020 census. Metro Olongapo, along with the municipality of Subic, is one of the twelve metropolitan areas in the Philippines. Though it has a notorious red light district reputation brought by the presence of night clubs catering to American servicemen, the city has gradually shifted into a more wholesome image. Olongapo is known for its culturally relevant sites – Marikit Park, Olongapo Lighthouse, Kalapati (The Dove Monument) and more. When in the city, check out these best things to do in Olongapo, Philippines.

ULO NG APO

The monument is of a village leader named Apo, who was highly regarded for his immense care and dedication to his tribe. He was a fair leader and a well-loved one.

MARIKIT PARK

Marikit Park is one of the earliest parks of Olongapo and still a local hangout place.It is a historical haven perfect for leisurely walks. This park’s distinct feature is the body of a U.S. Navy plane, originally displayed at the Subic Naval Base and acquired by the city of Olongapo.

TATLONG KRUS

A pilgrimage site in Olongapo City during the holy week turned into a famous hiking destination. It is located in Sitio Tabacuhan Brgy. Sta. Rita Olongapo City on top of Balimpuyo Ridge. The three crosses were constructed on a rocky part of the ridge facing the valley of Sta.Rita.

PAROLA

The Olongapo Lighthouse Parola, otherwise known as the Old Subic Lighthouse is located along the Olongapo National Highway just before the public cemetery on the hillside this eye-catching lighthouse still stands tall and proud, giving you a breathtaking view of the Subic Bay.

KALAPATI

Another landmark that mirrors the significance of the city in war history is the World Peace Memorial. This Olongapo City tourist attraction features the Dove monument that was built in memory of the lives lost during the tragic battle at Zigzag Pass. The battle is said to be one of the longest and most tragic battles during the World War II. The monument was built at the junction of the national highway and entry road to New Cabalan.

RIZAL TRIANGLE

This is the replica of the famous Luneta Park in Manila wherein Dr. Jose Rizal our National Hero statue is located. There is also a multi-purpose court within this triangular structure where most of Olongapo City's festivities are being held.

CITY BEACH

Beaches are one of the highlights, specifically for out-of-town guests. Notable beaches in Olongapo include the Driftwood Beach, Baloy Beach, and Many Resorts and Hotel. Enjoy a sun-kissed experience in Olongapo’s well-loved beaches. Olongapo’s sandy shores are right in the city with scenic views of the mountains.

CITY MALLS

SM City Olongapo Downtown is the first SM Supermall in Zambales and the first SM Supermall to open in 2012. In 2019 Olongapo have a new mall, SM City Olongapo Central is the newest Lifestyle and Malling Destination in Zambales.
